# My Research Interests

My broad research interests lie in <span style="color:lightcoral;">computational genomics</span> with the goal of gaining a greater understanding of genome biology. My previous research aimed to use <span style="color:lightcoral;">comparative genomics</span>, <span style="color:lightcoral;">multi-scale omics</span>, <span style="color:lightcoral;">computational biology</span>, and <span style="color:lightcoral;">chromosome biology</span> to identify and study genomic elements involved in:

- <span style="color:lightcoral;">Genome organization</span>, evolution, function, sex determination, and population diversity.  

I have employed a diverse set of rapidly evolving <span style="color:lightcoral;">next-generation sequencing (NGS)</span> technologies and computational methodologies for drafting and characterizing genome sequences in efforts to establish vertebrate species as models for studying genome biology. I have been involved in applying broad NGS techniques to both model and non-model systems to understand the dynamic nature of the genome in the following areas:

- **<span style="color:lightcoral;">Origin and evolution of Supernumerary (B) and sex chromosomes</span>**
- **<span style="color:lightcoral;">Organization of repetitive elements (satellite DNA and Transposable elements) in vertebrate genomes</span>**
- **<span style="color:lightcoral;">Genome assembly and annotation</span>**
- **<span style="color:lightcoral;">Genome-wide SNPs and population diversity</span>**

My present research involves developing bioinformatic and computational pipelines to study disease-associated non-coding genomic elements. Current projects include:

- "<span style="color:lightcoral;">Dissecting the Role of ‘Repeatome’ in Cancer Genomes</span>"
- Developing Bioinformatic Methods for Analysis of <span style="color:lightcoral;">TE Expression and Regulation</span> by <span style="color:lightcoral;">Short- and Long-Reads Sequencing</span>.
